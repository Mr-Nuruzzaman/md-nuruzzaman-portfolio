## The problem & my role

A brokerage cannot legally open an account until the client's identity is verified against their National ID. Done by hand, that means someone reading NID cards, retyping fields, and eyeballing them against records — slow, error-prone, and sitting at the most compliance-critical point of the onboarding funnel. At BRAC EPL Stock Brokerage I built the service that automates the whole step end to end.

This is an internal enterprise service with no public link, so this write-up stays at the level of what I actually built rather than screenshots or live numbers.

## How it works

The brokerage platform's onboarding service makes one call to this service with the **front and back images of the client's NID card**. From there the service owns the full verification flow:

1. **Extraction** — an EasyOCR-based pipeline reads both card images and extracts the identity fields off the document.
2. **Verification against the Election Commission** — the extracted data is sent to the EC's NID verification provider and matched against the Election Commission's real registration data, so verification is against the authoritative source rather than just what's printed on the card.
3. **Persistence** — the extracted data and the verification outcome are stored.
4. **Response** — the service returns a proper verification status to the onboarding service, which decides how the client's application proceeds.

## Approach & key decisions

**A standalone service, not a module.** Identity verification has its own dependencies (image processing, an external government-data provider), its own failure modes, and its own change cadence. Keeping it as a separate service the brokerage platform calls over REST means onboarding logic stays clean — it asks one question ("is this identity verified?") and gets one status back — and the extraction/verification internals can evolve without touching the platform.

**Python + EasyOCR for the extraction, FastAPI at the edge.** Reading structured fields off card images is an image-processing problem, and Python is where the practical tooling for that lives — EasyOCR handles the recognition (including Bangla-script text on NID cards), and FastAPI serves the verification endpoint the platform calls. The alternative — forcing extraction into the JVM stack of the calling platform — would have coupled the hardest-to-change part of the pipeline to the codebase least suited to it.

**Verify against the source, not the card.** Extraction alone only proves what the card *says*. The service's job is the match against the Election Commission's registration data via the EC verification provider — that's what makes the returned status meaningful for e-KYC compliance.

## Impact

Because this is an internal system, I don't have public metrics to point to, and I won't invent any. The substance: identity verification — previously the most manual, compliance-sensitive step in opening a brokerage account — became a single automated API call that extracts, verifies against government records, stores, and answers with a status.

## What I'd do differently

I'd invest earlier in a labelled evaluation set for the extractor — a corpus of NID samples with ground-truth fields — so extraction changes could be scored against a benchmark instead of spot-checked. For a component whose whole job is accuracy, a regression harness is the difference between "seems better" and "is better."
